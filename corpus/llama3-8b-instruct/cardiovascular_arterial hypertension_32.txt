**Chief Complaint:**

John Doe, a 58-year-old male, was admitted to the cardiovascular unit with a chief complaint of hypertension. He presented with a history of hypertension, which was initially diagnosed 5 years ago. The patient's blood pressure had been well-controlled on his previous medications, but he had experienced a recent increase in blood pressure readings.

**History of Present Illness:**

The patient reported a gradual increase in blood pressure over the past 6 months, with readings ranging from 150/90 mmHg to 170/100 mmHg. He denied any significant changes in his lifestyle, diet, or physical activity. He did report a 10-pound weight gain over the past year.

**Physical Examination:**

Upon admission, the patient's vital signs included a blood pressure of 160/100 mmHg, heart rate of 70 beats per minute, and oxygen saturation of 98% on room air. The physical examination revealed a blood pressure of 160/100 mmHg in both arms, with a normal cardiac examination. The patient's height was 5'10" (178 cm), and his weight was 190 pounds (86 kg).

**Diagnostic Tests:**

The patient underwent a series of diagnostic tests, including:

* Sphygmomanometry: The patient's blood pressure was measured using a properly sized BP cuff, and readings were taken at different times under specific conditions. The average blood pressure reading was 158/98 mmHg.
* Urinalysis: The patient's urinalysis revealed no abnormalities.
* Lipid panel: The patient's lipid panel showed a total cholesterol level of 200 mg/dL, LDL cholesterol level of 120 mg/dL, and HDL cholesterol level of 40 mg/dL.
* Metabolic panel: The patient's metabolic panel showed a normal potassium level.
* Fasting plasma glucose: The patient's fasting plasma glucose level was 100 mg/dL.
* ECG: The patient's ECG showed a normal sinus rhythm with a heart rate of 70 beats per minute.

**Diagnosis:**

Based on the patient's history, physical examination, and diagnostic tests, the diagnosis of arterial hypertension was confirmed.

**Treatment:**

The patient was started on a medication regimen aimed at achieving a blood pressure goal of < 130/80 mmHg. The patient was prescribed:

* Lisinopril (Zestril), an ACE inhibitor, 20 mg orally daily
* Hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ), a diuretic, 25 mg orally daily
* Amlodipine (Norvasc), a calcium channel blocker, 5 mg orally daily

The patient was advised to increase his physical activity, lose weight, and adopt a healthy diet. He was also instructed to reduce his sodium intake to < 1500 mg/day.

**Discharge Instructions:**

The patient was instructed to:

* Take his medications as directed
* Monitor his blood pressure at home using a properly calibrated sphygmomanometer
* Recognize symptoms of potential complications, such as chest pain, shortness of breath, or severe headache
* Understand the importance of adherence to therapy and lifestyle modifications for effective management of hypertension
* Schedule follow-up appointments with his primary care physician and cardiologist

**Follow-up Care:**

The patient will be seen in follow-up appointments every 2 weeks for the first month, then every 4 weeks for the next 3 months. His blood pressure will be re-evaluated at each visit, and adjustments to his medication regimen will be considered as necessary.

**Conclusion:**

John Doe was discharged from the cardiovascular unit with a diagnosis of arterial hypertension and a medication regimen aimed at achieving a blood pressure goal of < 130/80 mmHg.
