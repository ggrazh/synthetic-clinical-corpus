**Chief Complaint**

John Doe, a 58-year-old male, was admitted to the cardiovascular unit on March 10, 2023, with a chief complaint of hypertension. He reported a history of hypertension for the past 5 years, with increasing blood pressure readings over the past year. He also mentioned experiencing frequent headaches, dizziness, and fatigue.

**Medical History**

John Doe has a medical history of hypertension, dyslipidemia, and obesity. He has been non-smoker for 10 years and has a family history of hypertension and cardiovascular disease.

**Physical Examination**

Upon admission, John Doe's physical examination revealed a height of 5'9" (175 cm) and a weight of 220 pounds (100 kg), corresponding to a body mass index (BMI) of 33.5 kg/m2. His blood pressure was measured as 160/100 mmHg in the right arm and 162/102 mmHg in the left arm, with a mean arterial pressure of 124 mmHg. His pulse was 72 beats per minute (bpm) and regular. His heart sounds were normal, and his lung examination was clear. His waist circumference was 44 inches (112 cm).

**Diagnostic Testing**

John Doe underwent a series of diagnostic tests to confirm the diagnosis of hypertension and rule out related conditions. These tests included:

* Urinalysis: Normal
* Lipid panel: Total cholesterol 220 mg/dL, LDL 140 mg/dL, HDL 40 mg/dL, triglycerides 200 mg/dL
* Metabolic panel: Normal
* Fasting plasma glucose: 100 mg/dL
* ECG: Normal
* Thyroid-stimulating hormone levels: Normal

**Diagnosis**

Based on the patient's medical history, physical examination, and diagnostic testing, John Doe was diagnosed with essential hypertension. His blood pressure readings were consistently above 140/90 mmHg, meeting the criteria for hypertension diagnosis.

**Treatment**

John Doe was treated with a combination of lifestyle modifications and pharmacological therapy. His treatment plan included:

* Increased physical activity: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week
* Weight loss: Target a weight loss of at least 5% of his body weight over the next 3 months
* Healthy diet: Follow a heart-healthy diet that is low in sodium, saturated fat, and added sugars
* Reduced sodium intake: Aim for a daily sodium intake of <1500 mg
* Smoking cessation: Continue to avoid smoking
* Home blood pressure monitoring: Monitor blood pressure at home twice daily and record results

Pharmacological therapy consisted of:

* Lisinopril (Zestril): 20 mg orally once daily
* Hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ): 25 mg orally once daily

**Follow-up Plan**

John Doe will be followed up with his primary care physician in 1 week to assess his blood pressure response to treatment and make any necessary adjustments. He will also be scheduled for a follow-up appointment with his cardiologist in 3 months to reassess his blood pressure and cardiovascular risk factors.

**Patient Education**

John Doe was educated on the importance of adherence to his treatment plan and lifestyle modifications. He was instructed on how to monitor his blood pressure at home, recognize symptoms of potential complications, and understand the long-term nature of hypertension management. He was also reminded of the importance of regular follow-up appointments with his healthcare providers.

**Discharge Instructions**

John Doe was discharged from the hospital on March 22, 2023, with instructions to follow his treatment plan and lifestyle modifications as outlined above. He was also instructed to return to the hospital if he experiences any worsening symptoms or concerns.
