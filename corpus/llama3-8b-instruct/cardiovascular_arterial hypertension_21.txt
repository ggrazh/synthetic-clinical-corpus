**Patient Discharge Summary**

**Patient Information:**

* Name: John Doe
* Date of Birth: February 12, 1975

* Discharge Date: March 20, 2023

**Chief Complaint:**

John Doe, a 48-year-old male, was admitted to the cardiovascular unit with a chief complaint of hypertension. He presented with symptoms of fatigue, headaches, and blurred vision, which had been worsening over the past 6 months.

**Medical History:**

John Doe has a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. He has been on medications for hyperlipidemia and has a family history of hypertension.

**Physical Examination:**

On admission, John Doe's physical examination revealed a blood pressure of 160/100 mmHg in the right arm and 160/105 mmHg in the left arm. His height was 175 cm, and his weight was 90 kg. His waist circumference was 110 cm. Funduscopic examination revealed retinal changes consistent with hypertensive retinopathy.

**Diagnostic Testing:**

Initial diagnostic testing included a complete blood count, comprehensive metabolic panel, lipid panel, fasting plasma glucose, and urinalysis. The results showed elevated blood pressure, hyperlipidemia, and mild renal impairment. Electrocardiogram (ECG) revealed left ventricular hypertrophy.

**Diagnosis:**

Based on the patient's medical history, physical examination, and diagnostic testing, John Doe was diagnosed with arterial hypertension.

**Treatment:**

John Doe was treated with a combination of lifestyle modifications and pharmacological therapy. Lifestyle modifications included increased physical activity, weight loss, and a healthy diet with reduced sodium intake. He was also advised to quit smoking.

Pharmacological therapy consisted of an angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitor, ramipril 10 mg once daily, and a dihydropyridine calcium channel blocker, amlodipine 5 mg once daily. The patient was also prescribed a thiazide diuretic, hydrochlorothiazide 25 mg once daily.

**Follow-up:**

John Doe will follow up with his primary care physician in 2 weeks to reassess his blood pressure and medication effectiveness. He will also be seen in 6 weeks to evaluate his progress and make any necessary adjustments to his treatment plan.

**Patient Education:**

John Doe was educated on the importance of adhering to his treatment plan and lifestyle modifications. He was taught how to monitor his blood pressure at home and recognize symptoms of potential complications. He was also informed about the long-term nature of hypertension management and the need for regular follow-up appointments.
