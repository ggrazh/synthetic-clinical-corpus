**Patient Discharge Summary**

**Patient Information:**

* Name: John Doe
* Date of Birth: March 12, 1995
* Admission Date: March 15, 2023
* Discharge Date: March 25, 2023

**Chief Complaint:**

John Doe, a 28-year-old male, was admitted to our endocrinology unit on March 15, 2023, with complaints of increasing thirst, frequent urination, and blurry vision. He reported a 2-week history of these symptoms, which worsened over the past week.

**Medical History:**

John Doe has no significant medical history, except for a recent diagnosis of type 1 diabetes. He has never been diagnosed with any other chronic conditions.

**Physical Examination:**

Upon admission, John Doe's physical examination revealed a tall, thin male with a normal vital sign profile. His oral mucosa was dry, and he had a slight tremor in his hands. His skin was warm and dry to the touch.

**Laboratory Results:**

* Fasting plasma glucose (FPG): 420 mg/dL (23.3 mmol/L)
* Glycosylated hemoglobin (HbA1C): 12.5%
* Complete Blood Count (CBC): normal
* Electrolytes: normal
* Renal function tests: normal
* Lipid profile: normal

**Diagnosis:**

Based on the patient's symptoms, physical examination, and laboratory results, John Doe was diagnosed with type 1 diabetes.

**Treatment:**

John Doe was started on a basal-bolus insulin regimen, consisting of:

* Lantus (insulin glargine) 20 units once daily at 12:00 AM
* NovoLog (insulin aspart) 10 units before each meal (3 times a day)

He was also prescribed metformin 500 mg twice daily for oral antihyperglycemic therapy.

**Dietary Recommendations:**

John Doe was advised to follow a personalized diet plan focusing on whole foods and high-quality carbohydrates. He was encouraged to eat frequent, balanced meals with moderate protein and fat intake.

**Exercise Recommendations:**

John Doe was recommended to engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week. He was advised to adjust his insulin doses or carbohydrate intake to manage hypoglycemia risk during exercise.

**Patient Education:**

John Doe received comprehensive education on diabetes management, including:

* Causes and symptoms of hypoglycemia and hyperglycemia
* Signs and symptoms of diabetic complications
* Importance of regular foot examinations and eye exams
* Vaccination recommendations, including pneumococcal, influenza, hepatitis B, varicella, and SARS-CoV-2
* Importance of regular professional podiatric care

**Discharge Instructions:**

John Doe was discharged with the following instructions:

* Continue basal-bolus insulin regimen as prescribed
* Monitor blood glucose levels 4 times a day and adjust insulin doses as needed
* Follow dietary recommendations and adjust as necessary
* Engage in regular exercise and adjust insulin doses as needed
* Schedule follow-up appointments with the endocrinologist for regular monitoring and adjustment of therapy

**Follow-up Appointments:**

John Doe was scheduled for follow-up appointments with the endocrinologist in 2 weeks and 6 weeks to monitor his progress and adjust his therapy as necessary.

**Summary:**

John Doe, a 28-year-old male, was diagnosed with type 1 diabetes and treated with a basal-bolus insulin regimen, metformin, and personalized dietary recommendations. He received comprehensive education on diabetes management and was discharged with instructions for follow-up appointments and ongoing management.
