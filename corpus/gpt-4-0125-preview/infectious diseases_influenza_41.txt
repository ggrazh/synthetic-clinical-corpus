**Admission Information:**  
John Doe was admitted to the hospital on September 20, 2023, with complaints of high fever, severe headache, muscle aches, and persistent cough. Given the prevalence of influenza in the community and his clinical presentation, influenza was suspected.  

**Diagnostic Assessment:**  
Upon admission, a rapid antigen test was performed, which returned positive for Influenza A. To guide antiviral therapy and rule out the need for antibacterial drugs, an RT-PCR test was also conducted, confirming Influenza A H3N2 subtype. Pulse oximetry indicated mild hypoxemia with oxygen saturation at 94% on room air. A chest x-ray was performed, showing no evidence of pneumonia, characterized by the absence of lobar or segmental infiltrates, suggesting primary influenza pneumonia was not present at the time of admission.

**Treatment Course:**  
John was started on Oseltamivir 75 mg orally twice a day within 24 hours of symptom onset. Due to the mild hypoxemia, supplemental oxygen was not initiated, but his oxygen levels were closely monitored. Symptomatic treatment for fever and muscle aches included acetaminophen 650 mg every 6 hours as needed, with strict avoidance of aspirin due to the risk of Reye's syndrome. Hydration was emphasized, with John encouraged to drink at least 2 liters of fluids daily.

Throughout his hospital stay, John's symptoms gradually improved. His fever resolved by the third day of treatment, and his muscle aches and cough showed significant improvement. Oseltamivir was continued for a total of 5 days, completing the course on September 25, 2023. No secondary bacterial infection was identified during his stay, negating the need for antibacterial drugs.

John Doe was discharged on September 27, 2023, with the following instructions:

2. **Complete the full course of Oseltamivir as prescribed.**
